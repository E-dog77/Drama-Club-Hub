Drama Games

Change!

People Needed: 3+
Works On: Improvisation, Timing and Rhythm, Emotional Range, Listening and Reacting, Adaptability, Collaboration, Confidence, Voice Control, Imagination, Resilience
Difficulty: High
One person is side stage while the others act out a scene based on a prompt. At any moment, the person offstage can shout “Change!” and the last line said must be changed to something else, encouraging fast thinking and keeping the scene alive.

Freeze Frames

People Needed: 7+ preferred
Works On: Creativity, Teamwork, Movement, Spatial Awareness, Coordination, Imagination, Discipline, Body Language, Expressiveness, Stamina
Difficulty: Easy–Medium
A leader gives a prompt and small groups work together to physically create a frozen image or "frame" that represents the idea. Focuses on shaping with the body and ensemble coordination.

Bang!!

People Needed: 5+
Works On: Reaction Time, Focus, Discipline, Listening and Reacting, Confidence, Voice Control, Presence, Stamina, Adaptability
Difficulty: Easy
Players stand in a circle. The leader calls out a name. That person must duck while the players on either side point and say “Bang!” at each other. The slower one is out. Includes fun elimination rounds and a final showdown.

Freeze Tag

People Needed: 3+
Works On: Improvisation, Movement, Creativity, Timing, Listening and Reacting, Characterisation, Spatial Awareness, Confidence
Difficulty: Medium–High
A few players act out a scene. Someone from the side can yell “Freeze!”, tap someone out, and take their place. They must then start a new scene based on the physical position of the frozen actors.

One Word Per Person Story

People Needed: 2+
Works On: Teamwork, Creativity, Reaction Time, Listening and Reacting, Voice Control, Adaptability, Articulation, Confidence
Difficulty: Medium–High
Players stand in a line and tell a story one word at a time. It forces players to listen carefully, stay in rhythm, and build a coherent story together.

Death in a Minute

People Needed: 3+
Works On: Improvisation, Fast Thinking, Teamwork, Characterisation, Creativity, Timing and Rhythm, Expressiveness, Voice Control
Difficulty: High
Groups plan and perform a scene in under a minute. It must include a death, a strange murder weapon, and a bizarre line of dialogue. Fast-paced and hilarious.

Yes, And...

People Needed: 2+
Works On: Improvisation, Collaboration, Adaptability, Listening and Reacting, Creativity, Emotional Range, Confidence, Voice Control
Difficulty: Medium
A foundational improv game where players build a story by always beginning their response with “Yes, and…” This teaches the art of agreement, support, and pushing ideas forward together.

Open Scripts

People Needed: 2+
Works On: Voice Control, Characterisation, Text Interpretation, Adaptability, Presence, Listening and Reacting, Timing, Confidence
Difficulty: Medium–High
Players perform a script with neutral dialogue (e.g., "Hi." "How are you?" "Fine.") but must act with clear character, relationship, and emotion. The same words can tell many stories.

321

People Needed: 2+
Works On: Ensemble Trust, Improvisation, Risk-Taking, Adaptability, Timing and Rhythm, Listening and Reacting, Stamina
Difficulty: High
Players create a 3-minute scene, then condense it into 1 minute, then 30 seconds, then 15, 10, and 5. Requires memory, energy, and lots of physical comedy.

Andy's Coming

People Needed: 6+
Works On: Energy, Movement, Listening and Reacting, Physical Awareness, Group Cohesion, Playfulness
Difficulty: Easy–Medium
Inspired by *Toy Story*, everyone moves around the room as toys. When the leader calls “Andy’s coming!”, everyone drops to the floor. Great for warmup or cooldown depending on pace.

Honey, I Love You

People Needed: 4+
Works On: Emotional Control, Confidence, Presence, Voice Control, Ensemble Bonding, Focus
Difficulty: Easy
One person tries to make another smile or laugh by saying “Honey, I love you, won’t you give me a smile?” while staying in character. The other must resist laughing or breaking.

Clap Your Hands Game

People Needed: 4+
Works On: Timing and Rhythm, Group Focus, Ensemble Energy, Listening and Reacting
Difficulty: Easy
Players stand in a circle and pass a clap around using eye contact and hand motions. The goal is smooth, rhythmical group coordination. Variations can increase difficulty.

Energy Circle

People Needed: 6+
Works On: Ensemble Bonding, Focus, Voice and Movement, Listening, Confidence, Control
Difficulty: Easy
A gentle game where energy is passed around the circle in the form of sounds and movements. Great for both warmup and cooldown depending on tempo and style.

Dramatic Fish

People Needed: 5+
Works On: Imagination, Energy, Playfulness, Physical Expression, Spatial Awareness
Difficulty: Easy
Players move around like dramatic fish: dramatically reacting to imaginary underwater problems. Light and silly, perfect for releasing tension and getting creative.

What Are You Doing?

People Needed: 4+
Works On: Quick Thinking, Voice and Movement, Creativity, Imagination, Listening and Reacting
Difficulty: Medium
One player mimes an action. Another asks, “What are you doing?” and the first must say something completely different. The second then begins miming what was said. It keeps going from there!

Mirror

People Needed: Pairs
Works On: Concentration, Physical Awareness, Focus, Ensemble Trust, Listening and Reacting
Difficulty: Easy
In pairs, one player moves slowly while the other mirrors their exact movement. Encourages sensitivity and teamwork. You can switch leaders partway or move silently together.
