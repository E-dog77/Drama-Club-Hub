Top 25 Improv Tips

  1. Say “Yes, and...” Accept your scene partner’s offer and build on it. This keeps the scene alive and moving forward.
  2. Make your partner look good Support their choices and don’t try to dominate. Improv works best when everyone lifts each other up.
  3. Stay in the scene Avoid going for jokes or breaking character. Commit to the moment and the story you’re creating.
  4. Establish who, where, and what quickly Set the scene early by clarifying relationships, setting, and actions. This gives the scene structure.
  5. Trust your instincts Don’t overthink your next line or move—go with your gut and keep things flowing.
  6. Avoid blocking Blocking is when you deny someone’s idea. Saying “no” stops the scene. Instead, say “yes” and expand it.
  7. Play characters, not caricatures Create believable, grounded people. Even in absurd scenes, make your characters feel real.
  8. Commit fully Whether you’re a dragon, alien, or janitor—commit 100%. Your confidence makes the scene work.
  9. Use your body and voice Physicality and vocal variation bring scenes to life. Don’t just stand still—express with your whole self.
  10. Mistakes are gifts If something goes wrong, don’t panic. Often, mistakes lead to the best, funniest, or most real moments.
  11. Don’t ask too many questions Asking questions can stall a scene. Make bold statements instead to keep things moving.
  12. Be specific Specific choices (“You always eat cereal at midnight”) are more fun and give your scene depth.
  13. Play at the top of your intelligence Treat every scene seriously. Smart, emotionally honest scenes are often funnier than silly ones.
  14. Use status Explore power dynamics between characters—it creates tension and interest.
  15. Give gifts Offer your partner details to work with (like a job, relationship, or emotion). It makes the scene easier for everyone.
  16. Let go of control Don’t try to plan everything. Trust your partner and follow the story where it leads.
  17. Silence can be powerful You don’t always need to talk. Pauses, reactions, and stillness can say a lot.
  18. React emotionally A strong emotional reaction can drive a scene more than clever words.
  19. Support the reality of the scene Even if it’s a wild or magical world, treat it like it’s real. That makes it believable.
  20. Watch and listen closely Good improv starts with observation. Notice tone, movement, and emotion in your partner.
  21. Keep it simple Scenes don’t need wild twists to be interesting. A simple, honest relationship can be gold.
  22. Avoid inside jokes or references Keep your scene understandable for everyone—even the audience who doesn’t know your group’s in-jokes.
  23. Play age, status, or gender boldly Don’t hesitate to take on a different identity. Confidence makes your choice clear.
  24. Let your character want something A clear goal or motivation helps drive the scene forward.
  25. End on a high Know when a scene has peaked. Ending strong keeps the energy high and avoids fizzling out.
