Drama Games

Select one or more skills to filter games by works on skill
  • Change!
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Improvisation, Timing and Rhythm, Emotional Range, Listening & Reacting, Adaptability, Collaboration, Confidence, Voice Control, Imagination, Resilience
    Description: One person is side stage while the others are on stage. Those on stage get a prompt (or randomly choose something) and they start acting. At any point the person side stage can yell “Change!” And the last thing said must be changed to something else, with the idea of saying whatever you think of first and keeping the scene flowing.
    Difficulty Level: High
  • Freeze Frames
    People Needed: 7+ is preferred
    Works On: Collaboration, Coordination, Spatial Awareness, Cooperation, Imagination, Discipline, Body Language, Expressiveness, Stamina
    Description: One person leads the game while everyone else gets into teams of 3-6 people depending on numbers. The person leading gives everyone a prompt and gives everyone a short time to talk with their group and figure out how to make that shape.
    Difficulty Level: Easy-Medium
  • Bang!!
    People Needed: 5+ people
    Works On: Listening & Reacting, Discipline, Confidence, Voice Control, Presence, Stamina, Adaptability
    Description: Everyone gets in a circle while the person running the game stands outside of it. The game leader says the name of someone in the circle. The person whose name was called out has to quickly duck down while the two people next to them try to “shoot” each other by pointing finger guns at each other and saying bang! Whoever says bang last out of the two is out. If you duck or shoot when you don’t need to, you’re out. If your name is called and you don’t duck, then you’re out. There’s always some practice rounds before you actually start eliminating people. When there are two people left they stand back-to-back and given a buzz word. Every time the leader says a word the last two take a step; when the buzz word is said they must turn and shoot each other. Whoever shoots first wins.
    Difficulty Level: Easy
  • Freeze Tag
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Improvisation, Coordination, Timing and Rhythm, Listening & Reacting, Spatial Awareness, Characterisation, Adaptability, Confidence
    Description: 2-5 people start a scene and at any moment someone side stage can shout “Freeze” and all on stage actors will pause and the person who yelled freeze will tap someone out and take their position. Once the two have swapped the person who just got on stage will start a new scene in the position that they’re in now.
    Difficulty Level: Medium-High
  • Puppets
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Collaboration, Coordination, Cooperation, Body Language, Spatial Awareness
    Description: Two or more people (no more than 4 in a group) are “stiff actors” and one person is the mover. The actors must act a scene BUT they can’t move, that’s where the mover comes in. The mover has to move the actors (the actors need to be flexible and help the mover to move them) to keep the scene going.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Time-warp
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Characterisation, Timing and Rhythm, Emotional Range, Adaptability, Confidence, Improvisation, Listening & Reacting, Voice Control
    Description: One person becomes the timekeeper, and the others start acting out a scene. At any time, the timekeeper may freeze the scene and move time forwards or backwards as often as they want causing the actors to make quick decisions about who their characters are.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • One Word Per Person Story
    People Needed: 2+
    Works On: Collaboration, Timing and Rhythm, Listening & Reacting, Voice Control, Adaptability, Articulation, Confidence
    Description: The group lines up and takes it in turns saying one word each, to make a cohesive story.
    Difficulty Level: Medium-High
  • 321
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Improvisation, Emotional Range, Confidence, Characterisation, Listening & Reacting, Voice Control, Collaboration
    Description: The leader will count down 321, then however many people choose to step forward and improvise a scene. If the same people step up for a second time they will continue their scene.
    Difficulty Level: High
  • Death in a Minute
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Improvisation, Collaboration, Characterisation, Emotional Range, Timing and Rhythm, Expressiveness, Voice Control
    Description: The group will have 30 seconds to plan a scene where: Someone needs to die, there must be a murder weapon that is not usually a murder weapon, and a weird word or phrase must be said. This all needs to happen in a minute, hence the name.
    Difficulty Level: High
  • Yes, And...
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Collaboration, Improvisation, Listening & Reacting, Voice Control, Confidence, Emotional Range, Adaptability
    Description: In pairs or a group, each person must respond to a prompt or line by starting with “Yes, and…”, building the scene rather than blocking it.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Press Conference
    People Needed: 4+
    Works On: Characterisation, Listening & Reacting, Voice Control, Articulation, Improvisation, Emotional Range, Confidence
    Description: One player is holding a press conference but doesn't know who they are. The rest of the group knows (e.g., “You’re a dinosaur who just won the lottery”) and asks questions to help them figure it out.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Emotional Party Guests
    People Needed: 4–6
    Works On: Expressiveness, Emotional Range, Characterisation, Body Language, Voice Control, Confidence, Collaboration
    Description: One player hosts a party; others arrive one by one with an exaggerated emotion (e.g., furious, overly excited). The host must guess the emotion through conversation.
    Difficulty Level: Medium, Not Played
  • What Are You Doing?
    People Needed: 3–6
    Works On: Improvisation, Timing and Rhythm, Listening & Reacting, Adaptability, Coordination, Confidence
    Description: One person starts miming an action (e.g., brushing teeth). Another asks, “What are you doing?” The first answers with something different (“I’m skydiving!”). The second person must then act out that new suggestion. The first person will leave the circle when the second person begins their turn.
    Difficulty Level: Hard
  • Pass the Energy Ball
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Improvisation, Voice Control, Cooperation, Expressiveness, Confidence, Collaboration
    Description: Mime a ball of energy with a sound and movement and pass it around. Each person must transform it slightly.
    Difficulty Level: Easy
  • Machine Game
    People Needed: Not specified
    Works On: Collaboration, Cooperation, Improvisation, Body Language, Expressiveness, Stamina
    Description: One by one, players enter the stage as part of a giant “human machine,” using sound and movement.
    Difficulty Level: Easy
  • Three-Word Scenes
    People Needed: 2 per scene
    Works On: Emotional Range, Voice Control, Articulation, Timing and Rhythm, Listening & Reacting
    Description: 2 people will create a scene from a prompt, however, each line of dialogue player 1 says can only be three words long. Scenes stay minimal and focused.
    Difficulty Level: Medium High, Not Played
  • Mirror
    People Needed: 2
    Works On: Coordination, Spatial Awareness, Body Language, Discipline
    Description: Two players face each other. One slowly moves while the other mirrors them as exactly as possible.
    Difficulty Level: Easy
  • Energy Circle
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Voice Control, Confidence, Listening & Reacting, Cooperation, Expressiveness
    Description: Players stand in a circle passing energy around with sounds and movement.
    Difficulty Level: Easy
  • Clap Your Hands Game
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Confidence, Spatial Awareness, Timing and Rhythm, Listening & Reacting, Discipline, Collaboration
    Description: Standing in a circle, the leader will clap when they feel the energy of the room is ready, and everyone must attempt to clap at the same time. Once everyone has experience with the game, let anyone do the clap.
    Difficulty Level: Hard
  • Open Scripts
    People Needed: 2
    Works On: Memorisation & Recall, Characterisation, Emotional Range, Voice Control, Projection, Articulation, Confidence, Expressiveness, Dramaturgy
    Description: Open scripts can be accessed on the internet, printed and handed out, people will practice scripts, then perform them.
    Difficulty Level: Easy
  • Honey, I Love You
    People Needed: Warmup for the group
    Works On: Confidence, Emotional Range, Voice Control, Discipline, Listening & Reacting
    Description: Standing in a circle, one person will go around and pick someone in the circle. They will say, ‘Honey, I love you, please give me a smile!’ The other person must either say ‘Honey, I love you, but I just can’t smile,’ or smile and assume the original person's role.
    Difficulty Level: Absolutely Impossible, unless you are Tom Cruise.
  • Gibberish Translator
    People Needed: 8 (4 actors, 4 voice actors – can be adapted for smaller groups)
    Works On: Voice Control, Timing and Rhythm, Listening & Reacting, Body Language, Imagination, Expressiveness, Confidence, Adaptability, Collaboration, Articulation, Presence, Projection
    Description: Four actors act out an activity in gibberish, while paired voice actors translate in real-time with improvised dialogue matching the tone and timing. The result is a chaotic and hilarious performance requiring teamwork and quick thinking.
    Difficulty Level: Medium-High
  • Andy's Coming
    People Needed: 4+
    Works On: Confidence, Listening & Reacting, Spatial Awareness, Cooperation, Presence
    Description: Participants walk around the space. When the leader yells “Andy’s coming!”, all players must drop to the floor and freeze like lifeless toys. Other commands include ‘Touch the Wall’, ‘Freeze’, ‘Come Together’. Anyone moving or reacting too slowly is out.
    Difficulty Level: Easy-Medium
  • Disney Recreations
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Characterisation, Voice Control, Expressiveness, Imagination, Emotional Range, Collaboration, Body Language, Projection
    Description: Players receive a Disney film or fairy tale source and recreate it quickly in a one-minute improvisation.
    Difficulty Level: Medium
  • Word Associations
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Listening & Reacting, Articulation, Timing and Rhythm, Collaboration, Confidence
    Description: Players stand in a circle. One player says a word and the next immediately says a related word. The goal is to keep the chain going without hesitation, repetition, or breaking the association.
    Difficulty Level: Easy-Medium
  • Emotion Telephone
    People Needed: 2+
    Works On: Voice Control, Articulation, Emotional Range, Listening & Reacting, Expressiveness
    Description: In pairs, students pass a neutral line back and forth, each time changing the emotion or vocal trait. The challenge is to stay clear and connected despite changes. Groups then reflect on vocal technique.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Status Walks
    People Needed: Whole group
    Works On: Body Language, Characterisation, Presence, Discipline, Confidence, Spatial Awareness
    Description: Participants walk showing different status levels (1-10) through posture, gait, eye line. Then they pair off and improvise interactions while maintaining those status levels, reflecting on power dynamics.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Emotional Symphony
    People Needed: 4+
    Works On: Emotional Range, Expressiveness, Voice Control, Collaboration, Imagination, Listening & Reacting
    Description: Each student is assigned an emotion and expresses it vocally and physically when cued by a conductor, who layers the emotions building an “emotional orchestra.” Variations include switching emotions mid-performance.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Zip Zap Zop (with Variations)
    People Needed: 5+
    Works On: Timing and Rhythm, Listening & Reacting, Focus, Adaptability, Coordination
    Description: Players stand in a circle and pass a beat using “Zip,” “Zap,” and “Zop” in sequence, keeping rhythm steady. Mistakes reset the game. Variations include changing tempo, silence, or adding character voices.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Rhythm Circle
    People Needed: 4+
    Works On: Musicality, Timing and Rhythm, Coordination, Voice Control, Listening & Reacting, Expressiveness
    Description: In a circle, players pass a rhythmic sound around, building patterns by adding beats and claps sequentially.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Stage Picture Sculpting
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Spatial Awareness, Collaboration, Body Language, Characterisation, Imagination, Presence
    Description: One student acts as sculptor, arranging others like statues to create a frozen “stage picture” that tells a story. Later, actors bring the sculpture to life with movement or scenes. A tool for understanding space and blocking.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Switch!
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Adaptability, Emotional Range, Listening & Reacting, Confidence, Imagination
    Description: During a scene, the facilitator calls “Switch!” and actors immediately change a key element — emotion, objective, relationship, or setting. Encourages quick thinking and full character commitment.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Hot Seat
    People Needed: 4+
    Works On: Characterisation, Voice Control, Improvisation, Emotional Range, Substitution (Hagen)
    Description: One student sits in the “hot seat” in character while others ask questions. They answer in role, improvising based on backstory and motivation. Builds consistency in voice, physicality, and emotion.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Text Detective
    People Needed: Small groups
    Works On: Dramaturgy, Collaboration, Memorisation & Recall, Confidence
    Description: Students receive a short piece of text and become “text detectives.” They hunt for clues about time, relationships, setting, themes, and tone. Groups present findings and perform interpretations based on analysis.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
