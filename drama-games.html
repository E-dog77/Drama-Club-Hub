Drama Games

  • Change!
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Improvisation, Timing and Rhythm, Emotional Range, Listening and Reacting, Adaptability, Collaboration, Confidence, Voice Control, Imagination, Resilience
    Description: One person is side stage while the others are on stage. Those on stage get a prompt (or randomly choose something) and they start acting. At any point the person side stage can yell “Change!” and the last thing said must be changed to something else, with the idea of saying whatever you think of first and keeping the scene flowing.
    Difficulty Level: High
  • Freeze Frames
    People Needed: 7+ (preferred)
    Works On: Creativity, Teamwork, Movement, Spatial Awareness, Coordination, Imagination, Discipline, Body Language, Expressiveness, Stamina
    Description: One person leads the game while everyone else gets into teams of 3-6 people depending on numbers. The leader gives everyone a prompt and a short time to talk with their group and figure out how to make that shape.
    Difficulty Level: Easy-Medium
  • Bang!!
    People Needed: 5+
    Works On: Reaction Time, Focus, Discipline, Listening and Reacting, Confidence, Voice Control, Presence, Stamina, Adaptability
    Description: Everyone forms a circle with the leader outside. The leader calls a name; that person must quickly duck while the two neighbors "shoot" each other saying "bang!" The slowest shooter is out. There are practice rounds before elimination begins. Final two face off in a quick reaction showdown.
    Difficulty Level: Easy
  • Freeze Tag
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Improvisation, Movement, “Yes, Let’s” Attitude, Creativity, Imagination, Timing and Rhythm, Listening and Reacting, Spatial Awareness, Characterisation, Adaptability, Confidence
    Description: 2-5 people start a scene and at any moment someone side stage can shout “Freeze!” Everyone freezes. The person who called freeze tags someone out and takes their position, starting a new scene.
    Difficulty Level: Medium-High
  • Puppets
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Teamwork, Movement, Flexibility, Coordination, Trust, Physical Awareness, Body Language, Spatial Awareness, Collaboration
    Description: Two or more players act as “stiff actors” who cannot move, while one mover manipulates them physically to continue the scene. Actors must be flexible and work with the mover.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Time-warp
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Creativity, Character Writing, Reaction Time, Emotional Range, Adaptability, Timing and Rhythm, Confidence, Imagination, Listening and Reacting, Voice Control
    Description: One person acts as timekeeper and can freeze and move time forwards or backwards during a scene, forcing actors to quickly adjust their characters.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • One Word Per Person Story
    People Needed: 2+
    Works On: Teamwork, Creativity, Reaction Time, Timing and Rhythm, Listening and Reacting, Voice Control, Adaptability, Articulation, Confidence
    Description: Players take turns saying one word each to build a cohesive story together.
    Difficulty Level: Medium-High
  • 321
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Teamwork, Spontaneity, Improvisation, Emotional Range, Confidence, Characterisation, Listening and Reacting, Voice Control, Collaboration
    Description: On a countdown, players step forward to improvise a scene, continuing the story with repeated groups stepping up.
    Difficulty Level: High
  • Death in a Minute
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Improvisation, Fast Thinking, Teamwork, Characterisation, Creativity, Emotional Range, Timing and Rhythm, Expressiveness, Voice Control, Imagination
    Description: Teams have 30 seconds to plan a scene where someone must die, using an unusual murder weapon and a weird word or phrase — all performed in under a minute.
    Difficulty Level: High
  • Yes, And...
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Collaboration, Creative Thinking, Building Scenes, Listening and Reacting, Voice Control, Confidence, Emotional Range, Adaptability, Imagination
    Description: In pairs or groups, players respond to prompts by starting with “Yes, and…” to accept and build on ideas rather than blocking.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Press Conference
    People Needed: 4+
    Works On: Guessing, Creativity, Character Work, Listening and Reacting, Voice Control, Articulation, Improvisation, Emotional Range, Confidence
    Description: One player holds a press conference without knowing their identity, while others ask questions to help them guess.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Emotional Party Guests
    People Needed: 4–6
    Works On: Expressive Acting, Reading Social Cues, Improvisation, Emotional Range, Characterisation, Body Language, Voice Control, Confidence, Collaboration
    Description: One player hosts a party; guests arrive one by one with an exaggerated emotion. The host guesses the emotion through conversation.
    Difficulty Level: Medium, Not Played
  • What Are You Doing?
    People Needed: 3–6
    Works On: Fast Thinking, Creativity, Listening and Reacting, Adaptability, Coordination, Imagination, Confidence, Timing and Rhythm
    Description: One mimes an action. Another asks “What are you doing?” The first answers with something different. The second acts out the new suggestion.
    Difficulty Level: Hard
  • Pass the Energy Ball
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Creativity, Physicality, Vocal Work, Coordination, Imagination, Expressiveness, Focus, Voice Control, Collaboration
    Description: Mime a ball of energy with sound and movement, passing it around and transforming it slightly each time.
    Difficulty Level: Easy
  • Machine Game
    Works On: Physicality, Sound and Movement, Teamwork, Creativity, Imagination, Body Language, Coordination, Expressiveness, Stamina
    Description: Players join one by one to form a giant human machine using synchronized sound and movement.
    Difficulty Level: Easy
  • Three-Word Scenes
    People Needed: 2 per scene
    Works On: Slowed Pacing, Thoughtful Choices, Delivery, Emotional Range, Voice Control, Articulation, Timing and Rhythm, Listening and Reacting
    Description: Two players create a scene with each line only three words long, focusing on minimal, thoughtful dialogue.
    Difficulty Level: Medium-High, Not Played
  • Mirror
    People Needed: 2
    Works On: Trust, Awareness, Focus, Movement, Coordination, Concentration, Body Language, Discipline
    Description: Two players face each other. One moves slowly while the other mirrors exactly.
    Difficulty Level: Easy
  • Energy Circle
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Vocal Work, Focus, Connection, Listening and Reacting, Coordination, Confidence, Voice Control, Expressiveness
    Description: Pass an invisible energy ball around the circle, transforming its sound before passing it on. The receiver repeats and adds their own sound.
    Difficulty Level: Easy
  • Clap Your Hands Game
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Focus, Awareness, Connection, Timing and Rhythm, Listening and Reacting, Discipline, Confidence, Teamwork
    Description: Standing in a circle, the leader claps when the group energy is ready. Everyone attempts to clap simultaneously. Eventually, anyone may lead the clap.
    Difficulty Level: Hard
  • Open Scripts
    People Needed: 2
    Works On: Script Reading, Memorisation and Recall, Characterisation, Emotional Range, Voice Control, Projection, Articulation, Confidence, Expressiveness, Dramaturgy
    Description: Practice reading and performing printed or online scripts to build acting skills.
    Difficulty Level: Easy
  • Honey, I Love You
    People Needed: Warmup for the group
    Works On: Focus, Emotional Control, Fun, Voice Control, Discipline, Confidence, Listening and Reacting
    Description: In a circle, one person approaches another and says “Honey, I love you, please give me a smile!” The other either says “Honey I love you, but I just can’t smile,” or smiles and takes the original role.
    Difficulty Level: Absolutely Impossible (unless you’re Tom Cruise!)
  • Gibberish Translator
    People Needed: 8 (4 actors, 4 voice actors; adaptable)
    Works On: Voice Control, Timing and Rhythm, Listening and Reacting, Body Language, Imagination, Expressiveness, Confidence, Adaptability, Collaboration, Articulation, Presence, Projection
    Description: Actors perform a scene using only gibberish. Paired voice actors outside the scene translate in real-time with improvised dialogue matching tone and timing.
    Difficulty Level: Medium-High
  • Andy's Coming
    People Needed: 4+
    Works On: Focus, Reaction Time, Physical Control, Listening and Reacting, Ensemble Awareness, Spatial Awareness, Coordination, Confidence
    Description: Participants move around until the leader shouts commands like “Andy’s coming!” to which players must immediately freeze. Slow or incorrect reactions lead to elimination.
    Difficulty Level: Easy-Medium
  • Disney Recreations
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Characterisation, Voice Control, Expressiveness, Creativity, Emotional Range, Collaboration, Imagination, Body Language, Projection
    Description: Groups receive a Disney film or tale to recreate through a one-minute improvisation.
    Difficulty Level: Medium
  • Word Associations
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Fast Thinking, Verbal Dexterity, Listening and Reacting, Articulation, Timing and Rhythm, Collaboration, Focus, Confidence
    Description: Players quickly say associated words to keep a chain going without hesitation or repetition.
    Difficulty Level: Easy-Medium
  • Emotion Telephone
    People Needed: 2+
    Works On: Voice Control, Articulation, Emotional Range, Listening & Reacting, Expressiveness
    Description: In pairs, students pass a neutral line like “It’s time to go” back and forth. Each time, they change the emotion or vocal trait (e.g., whisper, joy, fear, sarcasm, monotone, shouting, sing-song). The challenge is to stay clear and connected despite drastic changes. Afterwards, groups reflect on clarity, breath support, and vocal technique.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Status Walks
    People Needed: Whole group
    Works On: Body Language, Characterisation, Presence, Discipline, Confidence, Spatial Awareness
    Description: Participants walk around the room showing different status levels, from 1 (very low status) to 10 (extremely powerful), using only their posture, gait, and eye line. Then, they pair off and improvise brief interactions (e.g., asking for directions, handing over a message) while staying in those assigned status levels. The group reflects on how posture influences power dynamics and story.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Emotional Symphony
    People Needed: 4+
    Works On: Emotional Range, Expressiveness, Voice Control, Collaboration, Imagination, Listening & Reacting
    Description: Each student is assigned a specific emotion (e.g., awe, fear, pride, nervousness). When cued by a “conductor,” students express their emotion vocally and physically. The conductor layers them in one by one, building to a full “emotional orchestra.” A second round can reverse roles or introduce switching emotions mid-performance. Excellent for expanding students’ expressive capabilities.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Zip Zap Zop (with Variations)
    People Needed: 5+
    Works On: Timing and Rhythm, Listening & Reacting, Focus, Adaptability, Coordination
    Description: Players stand in a circle and pass a beat using the words “Zip,” “Zap,” and “Zop” in sequence. The rhythm must stay steady, and mistakes (hesitations, incorrect words, out-of-sequence responses) reset the game. Variations include changing tempo, playing in silence, or adding character voices/emotions to each word. A foundational rhythm game used to sharpen reaction and ensemble timing.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Musicality – Rhythm Circle
    People Needed: 4+
    Works On: Musicality, Timing and Rhythm, Coordination, Voice Control, Listening & Reacting, Expressiveness
    Description: In a circle, players pass a rhythmic sound (clap, stomp, vocal beat, etc.) around. Adding to the sound, e.g. stomp, add another stomp so it is stomp, stomp, then a clap so it is stomp, stomp, clap, and so on.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Stage Picture Sculpting
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Spatial Awareness, Collaboration, Body Language, Characterisation, Imagination, Presence
    Description: One student acts as a sculptor, arranging others like statues to create a frozen “stage picture” that tells a story. Focus on levels, balance, shape, and emotion. Later, actors bring the sculpture to life with slow movement or short scenes. A great tool for understanding space, stage presence, and blocking.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Adaptability – Switch!
    People Needed: 3+
    Works On: Adaptability, Emotional Range, Listening & Reacting, Confidence, Imagination
    Description: During a scene, the facilitator calls out “Switch!” and actors must immediately change a key element—emotion, objective, relationship, or even setting. Encourages quick thinking and full character commitment while staying present in the moment. Excellent for pushing improvisers beyond their comfort zones.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Hot Seat
    People Needed: 4+
    Works On: Characterisation, Voice Control, Imagination, Emotional Range, Substitution (Hagen)
    Description: One student sits in the “hot seat” in character while the others ask them questions. They must answer in role, improvising responses based on their backstory, relationships, and motivation. Builds consistency in voice, physicality, and emotional truth. Great for scripted or improvised characters.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
  • Text Detective
    People Needed: Small groups
    Works On: Dramaturgy, Collaboration, Memorisation and Recall, Confidence, Subtext
    Description: Students receive a short piece of text (monologue or scene) and become “text detectives.” They hunt for clues about time period, relationship dynamics, setting, themes, and tone. Groups present their findings and perform an interpretation based on their analysis. Builds deeper understanding and smarter choices in performance.
    Difficulty Level: Not Played
