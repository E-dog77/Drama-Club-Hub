Improv Tips

  1. Say “Yes, and…” Accept your scene partner’s offer and build on it. This keeps the scene alive and moving forward.
  2. Make your partner look good Support their choices and don’t try to dominate. Improv works best when everyone lifts each other up.
  3. Stay in the scene Avoid going for jokes or breaking character. Commit to the moment and the story you’re creating.
  4. Establish who, where, and what quickly Set the scene early by clarifying relationships, setting, and actions. This gives the scene structure.
  5. Trust your instincts Don’t overthink your next line or move—go with your gut and keep things flowing.
  6. Avoid blocking Blocking is when you deny someone’s idea. Saying “no” stops the scene. Instead, say “yes” and expand it.
  7. Play characters, not caricatures Create believable, grounded people. Even in absurd scenes, make your characters feel real.
  8. Commit fully Whether you’re a dragon, alien, or janitor—commit 100%. Your confidence makes the scene work.
  9. Use your body and voice Physicality and vocal variation bring scenes to life. Don’t just stand still—express with your whole self.
  10. Mistakes are gifts If something goes wrong, don’t panic. Often, mistakes lead to the best, funniest, or most real moments.
  11. Be present and listen Truly focus on your scene partners and the moment. Don’t plan ahead while others are speaking.
  12. Take risks Improv rewards bold choices and vulnerability. Don’t be afraid to try something new or unusual.
  13. Use silence effectively Silence can add tension, humor, or emotional weight. Don’t rush to fill every pause.
  14. Ask questions When unsure, ask questions to clarify and build the scene naturally.
  15. Be specific The more specific your choices, the clearer the scene and characters become.
  16. Find the game of the scene Look for the funniest or most interesting pattern or idea and play it up.
  17. Keep scenes active Move the action forward with new information, choices, or conflicts.
  18. Use status Play with power dynamics between characters to add depth and humor.
  19. Keep your offers simple Complex offers can confuse your scene partners; keep it easy to build on.
  20. Give and take Share focus on stage by supporting others and also stepping up when needed.
  21. Remember: It’s not about being funny Improv is about truth, connection, and storytelling.
  22. Body language is key Use posture, gestures, and movement to show emotion and character.
  23. Play the moment, not the outcome Focus on what’s happening now, not where the scene might end.
  24. Respect boundaries Be mindful of content and comfort zones, especially in group settings.
  25. Have fun! Enjoy the process and support your fellow improvisers.
