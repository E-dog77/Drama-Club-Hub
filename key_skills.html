Key Drama Skills

1. Presence

The ability to command attention on stage through energy, focus, and awareness.

2. Voice Control

Using pitch, volume, and breath intentionally to suit a character or emotion.

3. Body Language

Communicating through posture, gesture, and movement to express character or emotion.

4. Emotional Range

Expressing a wide spectrum of emotions truthfully and consistently.

5. Timing and Rhythm

Mastering pace and delivery to enhance comedy, drama, or physical action.

6. Listening and Reacting

Responding authentically to others by staying present in the scene.

7. Memorisation and Recall

Learning and performing lines, movement, and cues accurately from memory.

8. Musicality

Understanding and using rhythm, pitch, and timing in performance and voice.

9. Coordination

Moving your body with precision and awareness—especially during choreography or physical scenes.

10. Stamina

Maintaining focus, energy, and performance quality over time.

11. Spatial Awareness

Knowing your position on stage relative to others for strong blocking and safety.

12. Discipline

Consistent focus, responsibility, and preparation during rehearsal and performance.

13. Adaptability

Adjusting to changes or mistakes while staying in character.

14. Collaboration

Working positively with others to create and improve dramatic work.

15. Resilience

Recovering from mistakes or criticism with a growth mindset.

16. Characterisation

Building a believable, consistent character through physical and vocal choices.

17. Imagination

Creating unique scenes, ideas, or characters from scratch or prompts.

18. Expressiveness

Using clear vocal and physical choices to show emotion and intent.

19. Dramaturgy

Understanding a script’s structure, context, and subtext to deepen performance.

20. Projection

Using proper breath and support to make your voice carry without strain.

21. Articulation

Speaking clearly and deliberately so every word is heard and understood.

22. Confidence

Taking bold risks on stage and performing with self-belief.

23. Substitution (Uta Hagen)

Connecting personal experience to a character’s emotions to deepen authenticity.
