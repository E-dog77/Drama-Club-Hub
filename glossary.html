Glossary of Theatre and Drama Terms

Articulation

Speaking clearly so every word is understood. Involves precise mouth and tongue movements and vocal control.

Blocking

Planned movement and positioning of actors on stage.

Breaking Character

When an actor steps out of their role, usually by laughing or reacting personally.

Characterisation

Creating a believable character through voice, body, and emotion.

Cue

A signal for an actor to speak, move, or enter/exit.

Duologue

A scene or conversation between two actors.

Ensemble

The full group of performers, often working together equally.

Fourth Wall

The imaginary barrier between the actor and the audience.

Freeze

All actors pause in place, often used to start or end a scene.

Gesture

A movement of the body or limb to express an idea or emotion.

Improvisation

Performing without a script, creating scenes on the spot.

Improvisational Prompt

A word, idea, or line used to spark a spontaneous scene.

Mime

Expressing meaning through movement without speaking.

Monologue

A long speech by one character.

Off-prompt

The side of the stage opposite the prompt-side — Stage Right from the actor’s perspective.

Projection

Using your voice clearly and powerfully to be heard.

Prompt-side

The side of the stage where the prompt (or stage manager with the script) traditionally sits — Stage Left from the actor’s perspective.

Rehearsal

Practice sessions for developing a performance before an audience sees it.

Script

The written text of a play or performance.

Stage Left/Right

Directions from the actor’s perspective when facing the audience.

Stage Presence

The charisma or magnetism an actor shows on stage.

Subtext

The hidden meaning beneath a character’s spoken words.

Tableau

A frozen stage picture that tells a story visually.

Upstage/Downstage

Upstage is away from the audience; downstage is toward them.
